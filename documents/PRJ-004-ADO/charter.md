## Objective

Launch PMO as Code publicly on a stable engine and standard, with a site that
documents everything that has shipped, evidence that the method governs its
own portfolio, and the beginnings of an external adopter community.

## Success Criteria

- 2 launch announcements (LinkedIn and Hacker News) published by 2026-11-15.
- Site documents 100% of released engine features at launch, with no released feature undocumented.
- 10 or more external adopters, measured by template forks plus action dependents.
- At least 1 external issue or pull request received on an ecosystem repository by 2026-12-31.

## Scope

In scope: pmoascode.com content, the template repository's adopter
experience, the launch announcements, the self-governance showcase page, and
an honest adopter count method. Out of scope: paid promotion, engine and spec
changes (their projects), and support commitments beyond OPS service levels.

## Milestones

- Site rewrite live (static, flat URLs, guidelines-clean): 2026-08-31.
- Site at feature parity with the released engine: 2026-09-30.
- Self-governance showcase live: 2026-10-15.
- Launch (gated on Engine 1.0): 2026-11-15.
- Adopter target reviewed: 2026-12-31.

## Risks

- Launch lands flat. Owner: John Tanner. Mitigation: the self-governing portfolio and live Refuge case study are the differentiators; both are verifiable by a skeptical reader in minutes.
- Adopter support burden exceeds capacity. Owner: John Tanner. Mitigation: OPS caps KTLO capacity; issue templates route reports; the standard makes behavior arguable from text rather than opinion.
